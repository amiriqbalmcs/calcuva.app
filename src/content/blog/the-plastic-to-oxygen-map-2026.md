## The Oxygen Cycle: A Marine Perspective
To understand the crisis of 2026, we must understand the "Biological Pump." Phytoplankton live in the sunlit "Euphotic Zone" of the ocean surface. They take in sunlight and CO2, and release oxygen—just like trees on land. But unlike trees, they also need to die and sink to the deep ocean to "bury" that carbon.

### How Plastic Breaks the Pump
In 2026, the sheer volume of plastic in the ocean has created a phenomenon known as "Plastic Snow." 
1.  **Smothering the Surface:** Large patches of floating plastic debris block sunlight from reaching the phytoplankton below. No sunlight means no photosynthesis, and no photosynthesis means no oxygen.
2.  **The "Ghost Buoyancy" Problem:** When microplastics are ingested by tiny marine organisms (zooplankton), they become more buoyant. This prevents them from performing their natural "Vertical Migration"—disrupting the entire carbon burial process.
3.  **Chemical Inhibition:** Leaching chemicals from plastics have been shown in 2026 studies to inhibit the growth rates of *Prochlorococcus*, the most abundant oxygen-producing organism on Earth.
