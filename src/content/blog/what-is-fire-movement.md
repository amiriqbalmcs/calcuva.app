In 2026, the concept of working until 65 is becoming obsolete for a new generation of professionals. The **FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early)** movement has evolved into a mainstream strategy for those who value time over luxury. 

But what is the actual math required to "retire" in your 30s or 40s?

## 1. The Core Philosophy of FIRE

FIRE isn't necessarily about never working again. It’s about **"Optionality."** It is the point where your investment income covers your living expenses, meaning you only work because you *want* to, not because you *have* to.

## 2. The 25X Rule: Your "FIRE Number"

The most famous formula in the FIRE community is the **25X Rule**. 
To calculate your FIRE Number (the total amount you need invested to retire), you simply multiply your annual expenses by 25.

**Example:**
If you need €40,000 per year to live comfortably:
`€40,000 x 25 = €1,000,000`

Once you have €1 Million invested in a diversified portfolio, you have reached Financial Independence.

## 3. The 4% Rule: Safe Withdrawal Rates

The 25X rule is based on the **4% Rule** (from the Trinity Study). It suggests that if you withdraw 4% of your portfolio in the first year and adjust for inflation every year after, your money has a 95%+ chance of lasting for at least 30 years.

**In 2026 Note:** Due to current market volatility and longer life expectancies, many FIRE practitioners are now opting for a more conservative **3.5% withdrawal rate** to ensure their portfolio lasts 50+ years.

## 4. The Three Types of FIRE

1.  **Lean FIRE:** Living a minimalist lifestyle to retire even faster with a smaller portfolio.
2.  **Fat FIRE:** Working longer to afford a luxurious retirement lifestyle.
3.  **Coast FIRE:** Having enough invested early on that you don't need to save another Euro—you just work to cover your current expenses while your "nest egg" grows on autopilot.
