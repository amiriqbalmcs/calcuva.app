Sizing a solar system in 2026 is no longer just about counting your light bulbs. With high-efficiency N-Type panels and hybrid inverters, the strategy has changed. You need to size for **peak load**, **surge capacity**, and the increasingly restrictive **net-billing export limits** enforced by local DISCOs.

## The "1.5 Ton AC" Benchmark: The New Standard
In Pakistan, the primary driver for solar sizing is the air conditioner. As temperatures soar, the cooling load dominates the electricity bill.
- A modern **1.5 Ton Inverter AC** consumes roughly **1,500W to 1,800W** during its initial startup and cooling phase.
- To run one AC comfortably during the peak sun hours without drawing from the grid, you need at least a **5kW Solar System**.
- If you plan to run two ACs simultaneously, an **8kW to 10kW system** is the recommended "sweet spot" for 2026.

## Understanding Your "Solar Window" and Production
In major cities like Lahore, Karachi, and Islamabad, you get roughly **5.5 Peak Sun Hours (PSH)** on average. This is the effective number of hours your panels produce at their rated capacity. 
- A 10kW system will produce about **55 units (kWh)** on a clear day.
- A 5kW system will produce about **27-30 units (kWh)**.

**Pro Tip**: Always size your PV (panels) 20% higher than your inverter capacity. This is known as **DC Overclocking**. For a 5kW inverter, installing 6kW of panels ensures you reach the 5kW output earlier in the morning and maintain it later into the evening.

## Net Billing vs. The Rise of Self-Consumption
In 2026, export rates (the rate at which the grid buys your excess power) have been reduced significantly compared to import rates. 
- **The Old Strategy**: Over-size the system to export as much as possible to offset the bill.
- **The 2026 Strategy**: Size exactly for your consumption. Every unit you consume directly from solar is worth Rs. 60+, while every unit you export might only be worth Rs. 15-20.

## Detailed Sizing Checklist for Pakistani Homeowners
1. **Accurate Load Logging**: Don't rely on guesswork. Use a [Solar Load Calculator](http://localhost:3000/calculators/solar-system-requirement-calculator) to list every appliance, from your fridge to your water pump.
2. **Shadow Analysis**: A 10kW system needs roughly **600-800 sq. ft.** of shadow-free area. Ensure no nearby buildings or water tanks cast shadows between 10 AM and 4 PM.
3. **Hybrid vs. On-Grid**: If you live in an area with frequent power outages, an **On-Grid** system will shut down when the grid is out. A **Hybrid** system with a battery bank is essential for 24/7 reliability.
4. **Panel Selection**: N-Type TOPCon or Bifacial panels are the 2026 industry standard in Pakistan due to their better performance in high-heat conditions.

Building a solar system is a 25-year financial commitment. Sizing it correctly today ensures you don't end up with an underperforming system or wasted export capacity tomorrow.
