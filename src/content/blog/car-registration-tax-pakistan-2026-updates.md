# Pakistan Car Taxes 2026: Why Registration Costs are Skyrocketing

The landscape of car ownership in Pakistan has changed dramatically in the last 12 months. With the introduction of new value-based taxation and aggressive federal withholding rates, the "hidden" cost of a new car—the registration—can now be as high as 15-20% of the total purchase price.

## The Shift to Value-Based Taxation

Historically, Pakistan used engine capacity (CC) as the primary metric for registration fees. However, in 2026, both the Punjab and Sindh governments have shifted toward **Invoice Value** assessment. 

This means that a high-tech 1000cc hybrid (which might cost Rs. 6 million) now pays more in registration than a basic 1300cc sedan that costs Rs. 4 million. This "Luxury Tax" approach aims to target high-income brackets while providing relief for entry-level commuters.

## The Non-Filer "Penalty"

The FBR has doubled down on its strategy to document the economy. For a vehicle above 2000cc, a **Non-Filer** is now charged a staggering **12% to 15%** of the car's value as an "Advance Income Tax." 

On a Toyota Fortuner or a Kia Sorento, this difference can amount to over **Rs. 1,000,000**. This makes it almost financially impossible for non-filers to purchase large-engine vehicles legally.

## Digital Registration: The ePay Revolution

On a positive note, the digital transformation of the Excise departments is nearly complete. In Punjab, over 95% of registrations are now initiated via the **ePay Punjab** app. This has eliminated the "Agent Culture" and brought transparency to the process. You can now calculate your exact fee, pay it through your banking app, and receive your smart card at your doorstep.

## What Should Buyers Do?

- **Verify the Invoice Date:** Dealers often delay invoices. Ensure yours is current to avoid late penalties.
- **Become a Filer:** The savings on even a small 800cc car (like an Alto) will more than cover the cost of filing your tax returns for three years.
- **Consider EVs:** Many provinces are offering 50% to 90% discounts on registration for Electric Vehicles as part of the 2026 Green Energy Initiative.
