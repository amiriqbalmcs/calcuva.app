Weight loss, maintenance, and muscle gain all come down to a single principle: **Energy Balance**. If you want to change your physique, you must first understand your body's energy "burn rate."

## BMR vs. TDEE: What's the Difference?

Your **Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)** is the amount of energy your body burns just to stay alive (keeping your heart beating, lungs breathing, and brain functioning) while at complete rest.

Your **Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)** is your BMR plus the calories you burn through physical activity—from walking to the fridge to running a marathon.

### The Formula for Change

- **Weight Loss**: Consume < TDEE
- **Weight Maintenance**: Consume = TDEE
- **Weight Gain**: Consume > TDEE

## Factors That Influence Your Burn Rate

1. **Muscle Mass**: Muscle is metabolically "expensive." The more muscle you have, the higher your BMR.
2. **Age**: Metabolism naturally slows as we age, largely due to muscle loss (Sarcopenia).
3. **NEAT**: Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis. This includes fidgeting, standing, and walking—it often accounts for more daily burn than actual gym sessions.
