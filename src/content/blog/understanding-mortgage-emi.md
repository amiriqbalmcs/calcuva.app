For most people in 2026, a mortgage is the longest financial commitment they will ever make. While the concept of "monthly payments" is simple, the math happening behind the scenes—known as **Amortization**—is where your wealth is either built or lost.

## 1. What is a Mortgage EMI?

Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. 

However, your EMI is not a flat fee. It consists of two components:
1.  **Principal Repayment:** The amount that goes toward actually owning your home.
2.  **Interest Payment:** The "rent" you pay to the bank for using their money.

## 2. The Front-Loaded Interest Trap

In the early years of a 20-year or 30-year mortgage, almost **70-80% of your EMI** goes toward interest. This is because interest is calculated on the remaining principal balance. Since the balance is highest at the beginning, the interest charge is also at its peak.

As you pay down the principal over the years, the interest component shrinks, and the principal component grows. This is why you barely see your loan balance drop in the first 5 years of a mortgage.

## 3. Fixed vs. Variable Rates in 2026

The 2026 economic environment has seen a shift in how banks offer rates:
- **Fixed-Rate Mortgages:** Offer stability. Your EMI never changes, making it easier to budget during inflationary periods.
- **Variable/Floating Rates:** Often linked to the central bank's policy rate. These can be cheaper initially but carry the risk of "payment shock" if rates rise.

## 4. The "Extra Payment" Hack

The most effective way to beat the bank is to make extra principal payments early in the loan term. Because amortized interest is calculated on the *current* balance, a single extra payment in Year 2 can save you thousands of Euros/Rupees and months of time in Year 15.
