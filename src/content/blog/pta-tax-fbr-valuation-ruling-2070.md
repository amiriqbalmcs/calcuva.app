# New FBR Valuation Ruling 2070: What It Means for Smartphone Users

The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) has recently shook the mobile market in Pakistan with the release of **Valuation Ruling No. 2070** in late April 2026. This ruling is critical for anyone planning to import a smartphone or register a gift from abroad.

## Why the Update?

Previously, many users were registering high-end devices by declaring them as "used" or "refurbished" to escape high duties. The new ruling sets a **fixed valuation** for 62 of the most popular models, ensuring that the government collects the maximum revenue based on actual international market prices.

## Major Changes for 2026

### 1. iPhone 17 and Samsung S26 Slabs
The valuation for the latest flagships has been set significantly higher. For instance, the **iPhone 17 Pro Max** now has a base customs valuation of $1,200, regardless of the purchase price on your receipt. This means the 18% sales tax alone will exceed Rs. 60,000.

### 2. The 6-Month Activation Rule
A key highlight of Ruling 2070 is the strict verification of the **6-month activation period**. If you bring a "used" phone that was activated less than 6 months ago, the FBR may treat it as a brand-new device, leading to a much higher tax bracket.

### 3. Filer vs. Non-Filer Gap
The government continues to use DIRBS as a tool to encourage tax filing. Non-filers now pay nearly **double the Withholding Tax** compared to active taxpayers. On a $1,000 phone, being a Filer can save you as much as Rs. 25,000.

## How to Prepare

If you are traveling back to Pakistan this summer:
- **Keep your Boarding Pass:** You will need it to prove your travel for Passport registration.

- **Avoid "Gray Market" Deals:** Many sellers claim a phone is "PTA Approved" but it may be registered on a stolen identity, which can be blocked later.
