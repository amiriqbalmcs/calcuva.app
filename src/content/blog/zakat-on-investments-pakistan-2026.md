# Modern Zakat: How to Calculate Tax on Stocks and Crypto in 2026

As Pakistanis move away from traditional savings and toward digital assets, the question of Zakat has become more complex. How do you value a volatile crypto portfolio or a long-term stock investment for Zakat purposes?

## Zakat on the Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX)

If you own shares in companies on the PSX, the calculation depends on your intention:
1. **Short-term Trading:** If you buy and sell for profit, you must pay Zakat on the **Full Market Value** of the shares on your Zakat date.
2. **Long-term Dividends:** If you hold for dividends, many scholars suggest you only pay Zakat on the **Company's Zakatable Assets** proportional to your ownership. If this calculation is too complex, paying 2.5% on the market value is the safest and most conservative approach.

## The Crypto Dilemma

Cryptocurrency is treated as a liquid asset. Whether you hold Bitcoin on Binance or stablecoins like USDT, you must calculate the value in PKR on your Zakat date and pay 2.5% on the total. Given the volatility of 2026, it is recommended to set a fixed date (like 1st Ramadan) and stick to it regardless of price swings.

## Real Estate and Plots

- **Personal Home:** No Zakat.
- **Investment Plots:** If purchased with the intention to sell for profit (trading), Zakat is due on the **Current Market Value**.
- **Rental Property:** No Zakat on the property value itself, but Zakat is due on the **Net Rental Income** saved at the end of the year.
