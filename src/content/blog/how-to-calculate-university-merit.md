Entering the 2026 admissions cycle in Pakistan can be overwhelming. With different universities using varied weightage for Matric, Intermediate, and Entrance Tests, knowing your "Aggregate" is the first step toward securing your seat.

In this guide, we break down the math behind the most popular university merit formulas.

## 1. The Standard Formula Components

Most universities in Pakistan use a three-tier system:
1.  **Matriculation (SSC/O-Level):** Usually 10-25% weightage.
2.  **Intermediate (HSSC/A-Level):** Usually 15-45% weightage.
3.  **Entrance Test (NET, ECAT, SAT):** Usually 50-75% weightage.

## 2. University-Specific Weightages (2026)

### NUST (National University of Sciences and Technology)
NUST places the highest emphasis on its own test (NET).
- **NET Score:** 75%
- **HSSC / A-Level:** 15%
- **SSC / O-Level:** 10%

### FAST-NUCES
FAST weightage can vary by campus, but the standard engineering formula is:
- **Entrance Test:** 50%
- **HSSC / Part-I:** 40%
- **SSC / O-Level:** 10%

### UET (University of Engineering and Technology)
UET follows the provincial policy which often prioritizes academic performance:
- **ECAT Score:** 33%
- **HSSC / Intermediate:** 50%
- **SSC / Matric:** 17%
*(Note: Always check the latest UET prospectus as these ratios can shift slightly year-to-year).*

## 3. How to Calculate Manually

To find your aggregate, use this simple math for each component:
` (Marks Obtained / Total Marks) * Weightage = Component Score `

**Example for NUST:**
- NET: 150/200 → (150/200) * 75 = 56.25
- Inter: 900/1100 → (900/1100) * 15 = 12.27
- Matric: 1000/1100 → (1000/1100) * 10 = 9.09
- **Total Aggregate:** 56.25 + 12.27 + 9.09 = **77.61%**
