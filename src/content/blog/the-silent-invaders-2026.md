## The 2026 Landscape: Where do Microplastics Come From?
Most people assume that microplastics are just "large plastic bottles that broke down." While that is one source (secondary microplastics), the reality of 2026 is that many are "primary microplastics"—particles that were designed to be small or are released through everyday friction.

### 1. The Laundry Leak: Synthetic Textiles
By 2026, over 70% of global clothing is made from synthetic fibers like Polyester, Nylon, and Acrylic. Every time you wash these clothes, the friction breaks off microscopic fibers. A single 6kg load of synthetic laundry can release up to **700,000 microfibers** into the wastewater system.

### 2. The Tire Dust Dilemma
One of the most overlooked sources of microplastics in 2026 is tire wear. As cars drive, the friction shreds small particles of "Synthetic Rubber." This "Tire Dust" is washed into drains during rain or becomes airborne. Our calculator includes a specialized module for high-mileage drivers to account for this significant leakage point.

### 3. The Personal Care Legacy
While many "Microbeads" in scrubs were banned in the early 2020s, 2026 has revealed a second generation of "Liquid Microplastics" in cosmetics. These are opacifiers and film-formers that are liquid in the bottle but harden into microscopic plastic films once washed off.

## The Biological Impact: Why Should We Care in 2026?
The danger of microplastics is twofold: physical and chemical.
*   **Physical Interference:** Marine organisms (and humans) can mistake microplastics for food. These particles can lodge in the gut, causing inflammation and preventing the absorption of real nutrients. In 2026, we've observed "Plasticosis"—a new disease in seabirds where plastic fibers cause scarring in the digestive tract.
*   **Chemical Sponges:** Microplastics attract and "soak up" persistent organic pollutants (POPs) like DDT and PCBs. When an organism ingests a microplastic particle, it is also ingesting a "Toxic Pill" of concentrated chemicals.

### Pillar 3: Tire Management
For the modern driver in 2026, tire selection is a sustainability choice. Choosing "Low-Dust" tires can reduce your contribution to airborne microplastics by 40%. Additionally, practicing smooth driving habits extends the life of your tires.
