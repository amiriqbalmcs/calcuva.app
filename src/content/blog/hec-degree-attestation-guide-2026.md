Whether you are heading abroad for a Master’s degree or applying for a government job within Pakistan, getting your educational documents attested by the Higher Education Commission (HEC) is a mandatory milestone.

As of 2026, the process is almost entirely digital, but it still requires precision to avoid rejection. In this guide, we break down the latest HEC attestation workflow.

## 1. Registration on the HEC E-Portal

The first step is creating a profile on the **[HEC E-Services Portal](https://eservices.hec.gov.pk)**. 

**Important Tips for 2026:**
- Use your **CNIC/NICOP** exactly as it appears on your ID.
- Upload clear, scanned copies of your original degrees and transcripts (not photocopies).
- Ensure your mobile number is registered in your own name to receive the necessary OTPs.

## 2. The Online Scrutiny Phase

Once you submit your application, HEC officials perform "Online Scrutiny." They verify your university's status and the authenticity of the degree format. This usually takes 2-5 working days.

During this phase, you may receive a "Deficiency" notice. Common reasons include:
- Missing Matric or Inter certificates (Equivalence is required if you did O/A Levels).
- Name mismatch between CNIC and Degree.
- Unclear scans.

## 3. The Math: CGPA to Percentage Conversion

One of the most frequent points of confusion is the **CGPA to Percentage conversion**. Many international universities and local employers (like FPSC or PPSC) require a percentage, but your transcript only shows a CGPA out of 4.0.

HEC has specific formulas for different universities (NUST, FAST, UET, etc.). If you get this wrong on your application, it can lead to issues during attestation.

## 4. Fee Payment and Submission (2026 Rates)

After online approval, you must pay the attestation fee. In 2026, the fee is typically:
- **Original Document:** Rs. 1,000 per document.
- **Photocopy Attestation:** Rs. 700 per document.

You can pay via **1-Link** (Mobile Banking, ATM, or EasyPaisa). Once paid, you will choose your submission mode:
- **Courier Service (Recommended):** Drop your documents at a designated TCS or Leopard courier center. They handle the logistics of sending it to HEC and bringing it back to your doorstep.
- **Walk-in (Urgent):** Available only for extreme emergencies at HEC regional centers (Islamabad, Lahore, Karachi, Peshawar, Quetta), requiring an advance appointment.

## 5. Final Step: MOFA Attestation

If you are moving abroad (especially to the Middle East, Europe, or the USA), HEC attestation is just the first half. You must subsequently get these documents attested by the **Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA)**. MOFA only recognizes documents that have been recently stamped by HEC.
