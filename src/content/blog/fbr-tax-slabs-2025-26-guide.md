The fiscal year 2025-26 has brought significant changes to the tax landscape in Pakistan. For salaried individuals, the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) has introduced a new progressive structure that impacts everyone from entry-level professionals to senior executives.

In this guide, we break down the math behind the new slabs, compare them with the previous year, and provide clear examples of how much tax you will actually pay.

## The Exemption Limit: Who Pays Zero Tax?

The good news for low-income earners is that the **tax-free limit** remains at **Rs. 600,000 per year** (Rs. 50,000 per month). If your total annual salary is below this threshold, you are not required to pay any income tax, though filing your tax return is still highly recommended to remain a 'Filer' and save on other transactional taxes.

## Detailed Slab Breakdown for 2025-2026

The Finance Act 2025 has structured the taxes into distinct slabs. Here is a simplified version of the most common brackets:

| Annual Salary Range | Fixed Tax | Tax on Amount Exceeding Lower Limit |
|---------------------|-----------|--------------------------------------|
| 0 - 600,000         | Rs. 0     | 0%                                   |
| 600,000 - 1,200,000 | Rs. 0     | 1%                                   |
| 1,200,000 - 2,200,000| Rs. 6,000 | 11%                                  |
| 2,200,000 - 3,200,000| Rs. 116,000| 23%                                  |
| 3,200,000 - 4,100,000| Rs. 346,000| 30%                                  |
| Above 4,100,000     | Rs. 616,000| 35%                                  |

## Real-World Examples: How Much Will You Pay?

### 1. The Rs. 100,000 Monthly Salary
- **Annual Income**: Rs. 1,200,000
- **Tax Calculation**: This falls in the second slab. 
- **Annual Tax**: Rs. 6,000
- **Monthly Deduction**: **Rs. 500** (This is a very low-impact bracket).

### 2. The Rs. 189,000 Monthly Salary
- **Annual Income**: Rs. 2,268,000
- **Tax Calculation**: This falls in the fourth slab (Exceeding 2.2M).
- **Fixed Tax**: Rs. 116,000
- **Excess Tax**: 23% of Rs. 68,000 = Rs. 15,640
- **Total Annual Tax**: Rs. 131,640
- **Monthly Deduction**: **Rs. 10,970**

## Strategic Tax Filing Tips
Being a 'Filer' in Pakistan is about more than just salary tax. It reduces your withholding tax on:
- Cash withdrawals.
- Property transactions.
- Vehicle registration.
- Dividend income.
