Albert Einstein famously called compound interest the **"eighth wonder of the world."** Those who understand it, earn it; those who don't, pay it. But what does the math actually look like for a modern investor?

## The Geometric Progression of Wealth

Unlike simple interest, which is calculated only on the principal amount, **compound interest** is calculated on the principal *plus* the accumulated interest of previous periods. This creates a feedback loop where your money starts making its own money.

### The "Cost of Delay" Analysis

Consider two distinct paths to wealth:

- **Investor A (The Fast Starter)**: Starts at age 25, investing **$500/month** for just 10 years, then stops entirely.
- **Investor B (The Late Bloomer)**: Starts at age 35, investing **$500/month** for a full 30 years until retirement.

Mathematically, at age 65 (assuming an 8% return), **Investor A will have significantly more money** despite investing for only one-third of the time. This is the raw power of the "Early Start Advantage."

## How to Maximize Compounding

- **Frequency Matters**: Compounding quarterly or monthly generates more wealth than annual compounding due to the increased frequency of interest recalculation.
- **Reinvest Dividends**: The engine only works if the dividends and interest are fed back into the principal.
- **Patience is Key**: The "Hockey Stick" curve of wealth usually only becomes visible after **Year 15**.
