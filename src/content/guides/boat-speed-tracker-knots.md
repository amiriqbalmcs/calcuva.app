### The Math of the Sea: Knots and Nautical Miles
Maritime navigation uses a unique set of units based on the earth's geometry. 
*   **The Nautical Mile**: Equivalent to 1.852 kilometers (or 1.151 miles), it is exactly one minute of latitude.
*   **The Knot**: 1 nautical mile per hour. 
Our tool handles these complex conversions automatically, allowing you to focus on the helm while we manage the telemetry.

### Expert Strategy: SOG vs. STW (Speed Over Ground vs. Water)
Traditional boat speedometers (paddlewheels) measure **Speed Through Water (STW)**. However, if you are in a 3-knot current:
*   **Heading Upstream**: Your STW might be 10 knots, but your actual progress (SOG) is only 7 knots.
*   **Heading Downstream**: Your STW is 10 knots, but your actual progress is 13 knots.
GPS measures **Speed Over Ground (SOG)**. This is the most critical metric for fuel planning and ETA (Estimated Time of Arrival), as it tells you how fast you are actually reaching your destination.

### Navigation Basics: Compass Heading and Track
Our Boat Tracker includes a **Live Compass Heading**. 
*   **Track**: This is the direction your boat is moving. In high winds or currents, your boat may "crab" (move sideways). 
*   **Precision Monitoring**: By comparing your physical compass to our GPS heading, you can detect "Leeway" (side-slip) and adjust your steering to maintain a straight course.
