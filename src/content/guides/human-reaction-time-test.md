## Neuro-Performance: The Science of Human Reaction Speed

### The Anatomy of a Click
When the screen turns green, your body undergoes a complex multi-step process:
1.  **Perception**: Your retinas capture the change in light.
2.  **Processing**: The signal travels via the optic nerve to the visual cortex.
3.  **Decision**: The brain sends a motor command to your finger.
4.  **Execution**: Your muscles contract to trigger the click or tap.
Our tool measures this entire chain in milliseconds, providing an objective snapshot of your "Neuro-Velocity."

### Expert Strategy: How to Score Under 200ms
To achieve elite-level scores, professionals use the following techniques:
*   **Peripheral Focus**: Don't stare directly at the center. Soften your gaze so you can detect the color shift across the entire screen instantly.
*   **The "Hair Trigger"**: Maintain a light, ready pressure on your mouse button or screen. Avoid "winding up" for a click.
*   **Hardware Optimization**: Use a wired mouse and a high-refresh-rate monitor. Disable "Blue Light" filters or "Night Mode," as they can slightly alter signal processing speeds.

### Factors That Slow You Down
Your reaction time isn't static; it fluctuates based on your physiological state:
1.  **Fatigue**: Sleep deprivation can slow your reaction time more than legal alcohol intoxication.
2.  **Age**: Reaction speed typically peaks in the early 20s and gradually declines by 2-6ms per year thereafter.
3.  **Distraction**: Even background noise can increase cognitive load, adding 10-20ms to your score.
