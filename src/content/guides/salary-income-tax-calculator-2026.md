## Understanding the New FBR Tax Slabs (FY 2025-2026)

The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) has implemented significant changes to the income tax structure for salaried individuals in Pakistan for the fiscal year 2025-26. These changes, enacted through the Finance Act 2025, aim to broaden the tax base and increase revenue while maintaining a progressive structure where high earners contribute a larger percentage of their income.

### Key Highlights of the 2025-26 Tax Regime

1. **The Rs. 600,000 Exemption**: The first Rs. 600,000 of your annual income remains completely tax-free. This provides essential relief to low-income earners, ensuring that those earning up to Rs. 50,000 per month do not have any income tax liability.
2. **The "Middle Class" Slabs**: For those earning between Rs. 1.2M and Rs. 2.4M annually, the tax rates have been adjusted to reflect the current inflationary environment. While the percentages might seem small, the cumulative effect on monthly take-home pay is noticeable.
3. **High-Income Surcharges**: In 2026, individuals earning above Rs. 10M annually are subject to additional surcharges. This is part of a broader "Equity Tax" initiative to ensure that the wealthiest segments of society contribute proportionally to national development.

### How to Calculate Your Tax Manually (2025-26)

To understand how our calculator works, you can follow the manual logic used by the FBR:
- **Step 1: Calculate Gross Annual Income**: Multiply your monthly gross salary by 12. Add any annual bonuses or taxable allowances.
- **Step 2: Identify Your Slab**: Locate where your annual income falls within the FBR's 6-tier system.
- **Step 3: Apply the Fixed Amount**: Most slabs have a "fixed" tax amount for the income earned up to the previous slab's limit.
- **Step 4: Apply the Percentage**: Multiply the remaining amount (the portion exceeding the slab's lower limit) by the slab's tax rate.
- **Step 5: Sum and Divide**: Add the fixed amount and the percentage amount to get your **Annual Tax Liability**. Divide by 12 for the monthly deduction.

### Tax Credits and Deductible Allowances
While the tax slabs are fixed, you can still lower your effective tax rate through several mechanisms:
- **Charitable Donations**: Donations to NPOs approved under Section 61 can yield a tax credit.
- **Investments in Pension Funds**: Contributions to a Voluntary Pension System (VPS) are eligible for a tax credit of up to 20% of your taxable income.
- **Profit on Debt**: Interest paid on house loans can sometimes be used as a deductible allowance under specific conditions for first-time homeowners.
