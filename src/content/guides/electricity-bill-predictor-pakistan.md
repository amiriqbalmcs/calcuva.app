## Understanding Your 2026 Electricity Bill

Electricity prices in Pakistan have transitioned to a highly progressive model. Understanding how your bill is calculated is the first step toward reducing your monthly expenses.

### 1. The Slab System (The Multiplier Effect)
Pakistan uses a **Progressive Slab System**. This means the more you use, the higher the rate for the *entire* consumption (in some DISCOs) or for the incremental units. In 2026, crossing the 300-unit and 700-unit marks triggers significant price jumps.

### 2. Protected vs. Unprotected Consumers
- **Protected**: If you stay under 200 units consistently, you pay a fraction of the standard rate.
- **Unprotected**: Once you cross 200 units even once in 6 months, you may lose protected status, and your base rate could double.

### 3. Surcharges & Taxes (The Hidden Costs)
Your bill isn't just about "units times rate." In 2026, taxes and surcharges can make up **35% to 45%** of your total payable amount:
- **Electricity Duty (ED)**: A provincial tax.
- **GST**: Federal sales tax at 18%.
- **Financing Surcharge**: A levy to cover the power sector's circular debt.
- **FPA**: Monthly adjustment based on global oil/gas prices.

### How to Reduce Your Bill in 2026
1. **Monitor the 200-Unit Threshold**: If you are close to 200 units, reducing just 5 units can save you thousands in taxes and slab jumps.
2. **Shift High-Load Appliances**: If you have a Time-of-Use (ToU) meter, avoid heavy usage during peak hours (typically 6 PM to 10 PM).
