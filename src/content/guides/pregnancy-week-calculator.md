Tracking your pregnancy journey is one of the most exciting experiences in a parent's life. However, the math behind "How many weeks am I?" can be surprisingly confusing. Most healthcare professionals count pregnancy from the first day of your **Last Menstrual Period (LMP)**, even though you aren't actually pregnant for the first two weeks of that cycle.

## How the Math Works: Naegele’s Rule

In 2026, most digital calculators (including Calcuva) use **Naegele’s Rule** to estimate your Due Date (EDC).
*   **The Formula**: Add 7 days to the first day of your LMP, subtract 3 months, and add 1 year.
*   **The Assumption**: This rule assumes a regular 28-day cycle with ovulation occurring on Day 14. 
*   **The Adjustment**: If your cycle is 35 days, you likely ovulated a week later. Our calculator allows you to adjust your cycle length to provide a more accurate estimation of your current week and due date.

## The Trimester Breakdown: A Developmental Roadmap

Pregnancy is traditionally divided into three trimesters, each with its own milestones and challenges.

### First Trimester (Weeks 1-12)
This is the period of rapid organogenesis. By week 8, the "embryo" becomes a "fetus."
*   **Key Milestone**: The heart begins to beat as early as week 5 or 6.
*   **The Challenge**: This is when most morning sickness occurs as the body adjusts to surging levels of HCG and progesterone.

### Second Trimester (Weeks 13-26)
Often called the "Golden Trimester." Energy levels usually return, and the risk of miscarriage drops significantly.
*   **Key Milestone**: "Quickening"—the first time you feel the baby move—usually occurs between weeks 18 and 22.
*   **The Scan**: The 20-week anatomy scan is a critical check to ensure the baby's organs and limbs are developing correctly.

### Third Trimester (Weeks 27-40+)
The focus shifts to growth and preparation for birth.
*   **Key Milestone**: The lungs complete their development, and the baby begins to "practice" breathing by inhaling amniotic fluid.
*   **The Final Countdown**: Any birth after week 37 is considered "Full Term."

## 2026 Prenatal Tech: Beyond the Calendar

As we move through 2026, technology has changed how we track pregnancy.
*   **Smart Wearables**: Devices can now track the mother's resting heart rate and sleep quality to predict signs of preeclampsia or gestational hypertension before they become dangerous.
*   **Virtual Reality (VR) Scans**: Many clinics now offer VR experiences where parents can "walk through" a 3D model of their ultrasound scan.
*   **AI Growth Prediction**: New algorithms analyze your weight gain, blood pressure, and ultrasound data to predict the baby's birth weight with 95% accuracy.

## Conception Date vs. LMP: The 2-Week Gap

If you know exactly when you conceived (perhaps via IVF or ovulation tracking), your "Gestational Age" will be different from your "Fetal Age."
*   **Gestational Age**: Used by doctors. It starts from the LMP. (e.g., You are "4 weeks pregnant").
*   **Fetal Age**: The actual age of the baby. It starts from conception. (e.g., The baby is "2 weeks old").
*   **Why does it matter?**: Ultrasound scans are the gold standard for dating. If your "LMP Date" and "Scan Date" differ by more than 7 days, your doctor will usually adjust your due date based on the scan.

## Preparation Checklist for Each Stage

*   **Early Pregnancy**: Start taking Folic Acid immediately. It is critical for neural tube development in the first 4 weeks.
*   **Mid Pregnancy**: Stay hydrated. Your blood volume increases by 50% during pregnancy, requiring extra fluids and iron.
*   **Late Pregnancy**: Prepare your "Hospital Bag" and finalize your birth plan.
