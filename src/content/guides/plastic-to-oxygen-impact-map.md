For decades, the conversation around plastic was focused on "littering" and "aesthetics." Today, we know that the stakes are much higher. Plastic pollution is a direct threat to the global oxygen supply.

## The Ocean's Role in Every Breath You Take
Did you know that **70% of the Earth's oxygen** is produced by marine plants and phytoplankton? While we often think of the Amazon or the great forests of the North as the "Lungs of the Planet," the reality is that the ocean does the heavy lifting. Phytoplankton, microscopic organisms that drift in the sunlit surface layers of the ocean, perform photosynthesis at a scale that dwarfs all terrestrial forests combined.

### How Plastic Disrupts the Cycle
When plastic waste enters the ocean, it disrupts this vital cycle in three major ways:
1.  **Surface Shading:** Large floating masses of plastic debris (like the Great Pacific Garbage Patch) block sunlight from reaching the phytoplankton below, effectively "turning off" their ability to perform photosynthesis and produce oxygen.
2.  **Chemical Inhibition:** As plastic degrades under intense UV light, it releases "Leachates"—toxic chemicals like Bisphenol A (BPA) and phthalates. Recent 2026 studies have shown that these chemicals can stunt the growth and reproduction rates of certain oxygen-producing marine bacteria.
3.  **The 'Plastic Snow' Smothering:** As microplastics break down, they sink to the ocean floor. This "Plastic Snow" covers the sea floor, preventing the natural exchange of nutrients and gasses between the sediment and the water column, killing the benthic organisms that support the entire marine food chain.

## How the Calculator Works: The Tree Equivalence Metric
To make the impact of your choices tangible, our 2026 model uses the **Tree Equivalence** metric. We believe that telling someone they "saved 5kg of plastic" is less impactful than telling them they "restored the oxygen output of 10 trees."

### The Calculation Logic
1.  **Diverted Plastic Weight:** We start with the weight of single-use plastic you avoid (bags, bottles, sachets).
2.  **Marine Vitality Factor:** Based on the region where your waste would likely have ended up (e.g., the Indian Ocean or the Arabian Sea), we estimate the area of the "Photosynthetic Zone" protected by your diversion.
3.  **Oxygen Translation:** We calculate the volume of oxygen produced by the protected phytoplankton density in that zone.
4.  **Tree Equivalence:** Finally, we convert that oxygen volume into "Tree Days"—how many days a mature, healthy tree would have to work to produce that same amount of air.

## 2026 Diversion Priorities: Where to Focus Your Energy
In 2026, not all plastic diversion is created equal. To make the biggest impact on the global oxygen cycle, our tool encourages you to focus on these "High-Risk" pollutants:

*   **Plastic Delivery & Shopping Bags:** Often too thin to be effectively recycled, these bags are the primary source of macro-plastic surface shading. They fragment into micro-plastics within weeks of entering the ocean.
*   **The Sachet Crisis:** In many parts of the world, small plastic sachets for shampoo, coffee, and detergent are the largest source of "unmanaged" waste. These are the primary drivers of "Plastic Snow" in coastal waters.
*   **Multi-Layer Packaging:** Chips bags and snack wrappers are made of bonded layers of plastic and foil. They are essentially "immortal" in the ocean and provide a surface for harmful bacteria to grow and travel across oceans.
