### The Ultimate Financial Scorecard
Your salary tells you how much money is flowing *through* your life, but your **Net Worth** tells you how much is actually *staying*. Whether you are saving for a business, retirement, or your children's future, tracking your net worth is the first step toward financial freedom. #### 1. The Asset Side: Building Value
Assets are things that put money in your pocket or hold value over time.
*   **Liquid Assets**: Cash, gold, and savings that can be accessed quickly.
*   **Appreciating Assets**: Real estate and investments that (usually) grow in value.
*   **Depreciating Assets**: Cars and electronics. While these are assets, their value drops every year. Use their **current resale value**, not the price you paid.

#### 2. The Liability Side: Managing the Weight
Liabilities are things that take money out of your pocket.
*   **Good Debt**: A home loan with a manageable interest rate that allows you to own an appreciating asset.
*   **Bad Debt**: High-interest credit cards or loans for items that lose value (like clothes or holidays).

#### Liquid vs. Illiquid: The "Accessibility" Math
In 2026, we categorize assets by liquidity:
*   **Liquid Net Worth**: This is your "Emergency Fuel." It includes cash, stocks, and gold that can be converted to cash in under 48 hours. If your net worth is 10 million but 9 million is in a plot of land that takes 6 months to sell, your "Liquidity Ratio" is dangerously low.
*   **Illiquid Assets**: Real estate, retirement funds, and private business equity. These build long-term wealth but don't help in an immediate crisis.

#### Inflation-Adjusted Net Worth (PKR 2026)
For our Pakistani users, a rising net worth in PKR may be deceptive if inflation is also high. 
*   **Purchasing Power**: If your net worth grew by 10% but the cost of living grew by 15%, your "Real Net Worth" actually decreased. 
*   **Diversification Strategy**: To protect your net worth from currency devaluation, we recommend holding a portion of assets in diversified buckets like gold, foreign-denominated mutual funds, or export-oriented business equity.

#### The "Invisible Asset": Human Capital
While our calculator tracks physical wealth, remember your **Human Capital**—your ability to earn future income. 
*   **Education & Skills**: Investing Rs. 500,000 in a specialized certification that increases your salary by Rs. 50,000/month adds more to your "Life Net Worth" than any stock investment.
*   **Protection**: Use our **[Salary Income Tax Calculator](/calculators/salary-income-tax-calculator-2026)** to see how increasing your earning power impacts your net savings.

#### The Debt-to-Asset Ratio
This is a critical metric for financial stability.
*   **Below 30%**: Excellent. You have significant equity and are "financially sound."
*   **30% - 50%**: Moderate. You are using leverage to build wealth, but need to be careful of interest rate hikes.
*   **Above 50%**: High Risk. More than half of what you "own" is actually owned by a bank. Focus on paying down debt via the **[Debt Payoff Strategy](/calculators/debt-payoff-calculator)**.

#### Expert Strategy: Increasing Your Net Worth
There are only two ways to increase your net worth:
1. **Increase Asset Value**: Save more, invest in appreciating assets, or wait for your property to grow in value.
2. **Decrease Liabilities**: Pay down your loans. Every Rs. 1000 you pay toward a loan principal increases your net worth by exactly Rs. 1000.
