### The Ultimate Financial Scorecard
Your salary tells you how much money is flowing *through* your life, but your **Net Worth** tells you how much is actually *staying*. Whether you are saving for a business, retirement, or your children's future, tracking your net worth is the first step toward financial freedom. Calcuva provides a **Private Financial Dashboard** to audit your life.

#### 1. The Asset Side: Building Value
Assets are things that put money in your pocket or hold value over time.
*   **Liquid Assets**: Cash, gold, and savings that can be accessed quickly.
*   **Appreciating Assets**: Real estate and investments that (usually) grow in value.
*   **Depreciating Assets**: Cars and electronics. While these are assets, their value drops every year. Use their **current resale value**, not the price you paid.

#### 2. The Liability Side: Managing the Weight
Liabilities are things that take money out of your pocket.
*   **Good Debt**: A home loan with a manageable interest rate that allows you to own an appreciating asset.
*   **Bad Debt**: High-interest credit cards or loans for items that lose value (like clothes or holidays).

#### The Debt-to-Asset Ratio
This is a critical metric for financial stability.
*   **Below 30%**: Excellent. You have significant equity and are "financially sound."
*   **30% - 50%**: Moderate. You are using leverage to build wealth, but need to be careful of interest rate hikes.
*   **Above 50%**: High Risk. More than half of what you "own" is actually owned by a bank. Focus on paying down debt.

#### Expert Strategy: Increasing Your Net Worth
There are only two ways to increase your net worth:
1. **Increase Asset Value**: Save more, invest in appreciating assets, or wait for your property to grow in value.
2. **Decrease Liabilities**: Pay down your loans. Every Rs. 1000 you pay toward a loan principal increases your net worth by exactly Rs. 1000.

#### Why "Net Worth" is Better Than "Salary"
Someone earning Rs. 500,000/month but spending Rs. 550,000 is technically "poorer" every month. Someone earning Rs. 100,000 but saving Rs. 20,000 is building a future. Net worth tracks the **Result** of your financial discipline, not just your income level.
