## Understanding PTA DIRBS Tax in 2026

If you are bringing a smartphone to Pakistan from abroad, you must register it with the **Pakistan Telecommunication Authority (PTA)** and pay the applicable taxes through the **Device Identification Registration and Blocking System (DIRBS)**.

### How the Tax is Calculated

The total tax is not a single flat fee. It consists of several components managed by the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR):

1. **Customs Duty**: A base percentage of the phone's value.
2. **Regulatory Duty (RD)**: A protectionist tax to encourage local manufacturing.
3. **Sales Tax (GST)**: Currently 18% for high-end smartphones in 2026.
4. **Mobile Levy**: A fixed fee based on the handset's price bracket.
5. **Withholding Tax (WHT)**: Higher for Non-Filers and lower for Filers.

### Passport vs. CNIC Registration

| Feature | Passport Registration | CNIC Registration |
|---------|-----------------------|-------------------|
| **Eligibility** | Valid international travel within 60 days | Any Pakistani Citizen |
| **Tax Rate** | Generally 15-20% lower | Standard Rate |
| **Limit** | One handset per calendar year | Multiple (subject to tax) |

### The April 2026 Valuation Update (Ruling 2070)

The FBR recently updated the **Customs Value** for popular models like the iPhone 17 and Samsung S26 series. This means even if you bought your phone for a discount abroad, the FBR will assess its tax based on their fixed "Valuation List."

### How to Check Your IMEI

Before paying any tax, always verify your phone's status:
1. Dial `*#06#` on your phone to get your IMEI.
2. Send the IMEI via SMS to **8484**.
3. You will receive a message stating if your device is **Compliant**, **Non-Compliant**, or **Blocked**.

> [!TIP]
> **Pro Tip:** If you are a regular traveler, always use your Passport for the highest-value device to save significantly on the Sales Tax component.
