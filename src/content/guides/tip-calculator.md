Tipping customs vary wildly by country, but in places like the United States, tipping is a standard and expected part of the service industry. Calculating tips and splitting bills can quickly become difficult at the end of a meal, especially with larger groups.

## Tipping Standards

- **10%** – Generally means service was below expectations.
- **15%** – The baseline standard for average restaurant service.
- **18 - 20%** – Standard for good to excellent service. Many modern point-of-sale systems now default to 18%.
- **25%+** – Reserved for exceptional service, fine dining, or very large parties.

## The Auto-Gratuity Rule

If you are dining in a party of 6 or more, always check the receipt before adding a tip. Many restaurants automatically add an "auto-gratuity" (usually 18%) to large groups to insure their serving staff are compensated. If you add a tip without checking, you may be double-tipping.

## Splitting Etiquette

When splitting a large bill:
1. It is usually best to split evenly if the meals were similarly priced. It avoids nickel-and-diming.
2. If one person ordered significantly more expensive items (like expensive cocktails or a multi-course steak), that person should volunteer to pay slightly more.
3. Use a tip calculator app to find the exact per-person value, ensuring the waiter receives the proper total amount.
