Understanding your battery backup is essential for surviving load-shedding and optimizing your off-grid solar system.

## The Backup Formula
To calculate backup time, you need to know three things:
1. **Total Energy (Wh)** = Battery Ah × Voltage
2. **Usable Energy** = Total Energy × Depth of Discharge (DoD)
3. **Runtime (Hours)** = (Usable Energy × Inverter Efficiency) / Load (Watts)

## Battery Types & Efficiency
- **Lithium (LiFePO4)**: The gold standard. 80-90% DoD. High efficiency, long life (10+ years), but higher upfront cost.
- **Tubular (Deep Cycle)**: Reliable and affordable. 50% DoD. Requires maintenance and has a shorter lifespan (3-5 years).
- **Lead Acid**: Cheapest but least efficient. 40-50% DoD.

## Pro Tips for Maximum Backup
- **Reduce Instantaneous Load**: Turn off heavy appliances like ACs or irons when running on battery backup.
- **Monitor Voltage**: Low voltage cut-offs protect your batteries from over-discharging.
- **Parallel vs. Series**: Ensure your battery bank voltage matches your inverter (12V, 24V, or 48V).
