### The Evolution of the "Ideal"
What is a "healthy" weight? While the answer is individual, medical science has spent decades developing **Ideal Body Weight (IBW)** formulas to provide a data-driven baseline for clinical health. Originally designed for calculating medication dosages in hospitals, these formulas—**Robinson, Miller, Devine, and Hamwi**—have become the global gold standard for setting fitness and weight loss orientations.

#### Decoding the Four Major Formulas
Calcuva's engine calculates your target using all four major clinical equations to provide a balanced "Consensus Range":
1. **Devine (1974)**: The original breakthrough formula. It remains the most widely used in medical environments worldwide.
2. **Robinson (1983)**: An update to Devine's work, providing more accurate targets for the modern population.
3. **Miller (1983)**: Often results in the most conservative (lowest) weight targets.
4. **Hamwi (1964)**: Known for its simplicity, it is primarily used in dietetics and nutrition offices for quick screening.

#### The Importance of the "Healthy Range"
No single formula is perfect because no two bodies are identical. This is why we display the **WHO BMI Range (18.5 - 25)** alongside the IBW results. A healthy weight is not a single point on a graph; it is a **15–20kg spectrum**. Your position within that spectrum depends on your muscle mass, bone density, and metabolic health.

#### Expert Strategy: The Frame-Size Correction
One of the most frequent criticisms of IBW formulas is that they don't account for "bone structure." You can refine your target by checking your **Wrist Circumference**:
*   **Small Frame**: Subtract 10% from the formula result.
*   **Medium Frame**: Use the result exactly.
*   **Large Frame**: Add 10% to the result.
This simple adjustment ensures that a naturally broad-shouldered individual is not chasing an unrealistic and potentially unhealthy low weight.

#### Case Study: The Proportionality Plateau
Many users reach a "Plateau" just 5kg away from their Miller target. Biologically, this is often the point where your body has found its **Set Point**. If your biomarkers (blood pressure, cholesterol, energy levels) are optimal at this weight, "forcing" the last 5kg can sometimes lead to muscle loss or hormonal disruption. Always prioritize **Health Markers** over reaching a theoretical formula number.

#### Technical Component: Height-Base Math
All these formulas follow a similar recursive logic: a base weight for the first 5 feet (60 inches) of height, plus a specific weight addition for every inch thereafter.
*   **Devine (Male)**: 50.0 kg + 2.3 kg per inch over 5ft.
*   **Miller (Female)**: 53.1 kg + 1.36 kg per inch over 5ft.
Notice that the Devine formula is more aggressive in its "per-inch" growth, making it a favorite for tracking taller individuals.

#### Beyond the Number: Functional Health
An ideal weight is only useful if it supports a high quality of life. If you reach your "Ideal" weight but have no strength or stamina, you have achieved **Under-Muscled Thinness**.
