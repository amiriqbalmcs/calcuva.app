### The Architecture of Freedom
Retirement is not an "Age"; it is a **Number**. In the traditional 40-year career model, we work until 65 because we don't know the math of independence. The **FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early)** framework shifts the focus from "working for money" to "making money work for you." Calcuva provides the computational roadmap to find your exact date of liberation.

#### The Three Stages of Wealth
To reach independence, an individual typically moves through three phases:
1. **The Accumulation Phase**: Where your [Salary](/calculators/hourly-to-salary-calculator) is your primary engine. Your goal is to maximize your "Savings Rate."
2. **The Inflection Phase**: Where your [SIP portfolio](/calculators/sip-investment-calculator) gains begin to equal your monthly expenses.
3. **The Withdrawal Phase**: Where you live off the "Dividends of Time."

#### Expert Strategy: The 4% Rule and the "25x" Shortcut
The "Rule of 25" is the bedrock of retirement planning.
*   **Target**: 25 x Annual Expenses.
*   **Safe Withdrawal Rate (SWR)**: 4%.
If you can manage your lifestyle on $5,000 a month ($60,000 a year), you need a **$1.5 Million** corpus. While this sounds like a massive number, Calcuva's [Retirement Calculator] shows how consistent compounding over 15–20 years makes it achievable for any disciplined saver.

#### Case Study: The "Sequence of Returns" Danger
The biggest threat to a new retiree is not inflation, but **Timing**.
*   **Investor A**: Retires in a bull market. Their portfolio grows 20% in Year 1. They are "set for life."
*   **Investor B**: Retires in a bear market. Their portfolio drops 20% in Year 1. 
Because Investor B is withdrawing money while the market is down, they are selling "cheap units," which permanently damages their portfolio's future growth. We recommend a **2-year Cash Buffer** so you never have to sell stocks during a temporary market crash.

#### Technical Component: Real Rate of Return
Our calculator uses the **Fisher Equation** internally:
`Real Rate = [(1 + Nominal Rate) / (1 + Inflation Rate)] - 1`
If the market returns 10% and inflation is 3%, your "Real Growth" is about 7%. Never plan your retirement using nominal numbers; if you do, your purchasing power will fail you in the final decades of your life.

#### LeanFIRE, FatFIRE, and CoastFIRE
Our tool allows you to simulate three distinct lifestyles:
1. **LeanFIRE**: Focuses on extreme frugality to reach freedom as fast as possible (often in 10 years).
2. **FatFIRE**: Focuses on high-earning and higher corpus targets to enjoy a luxury retirement.
3. **CoastFIRE**: You save enough early on (e.g., $200k by age 30) that you never need to save another dollar. Even without further contributions, that money will "Coast" its way to $1.5M by age 65.

#### Managing Healthcare and Taxes
Retirement planning must account for the "Silent Expenses." 
*   **Health Insurance**: In many countries, this is the largest expense for retirees under 65.
*   **Tax Buckets**: Are you withdrawing from a taxable brokerage or a tax-free retirement fund?
