Google's ranking algorithms use "Topical Completeness" as a major signal for Expertise (E-E-A-T).
*   **Thin Content (<300 words)**: Often flagged as low-value unless it's a very specific answer.
*   **Standard Content (600 - 1,000 words)**: Good for news and quick tutorials.
*   **Authority Content (1,500+ words)**: The sweet spot for ranking on the first page of Google for competitive keywords.

#### Technical Component: Word vs. Character Logic
Our counter uses a high-performance **Regex (Regular Expression)** engine:
*   **Words**: Defined as any sequence of characters separated by whitespace or punctuation.
*   **Characters**: Every individual glyph, including emojis, special symbols, and spaces.
*   **Sentences**: Identified by terminal punctuation (. ! ?).
Understanding these metrics allows you to monitor your **Average Sentence Length**, a critical factor in the Flesch-Kincaid Readability Score. Aim for 15–20 words per sentence for maximum reader retention.

#### Managing Content Density and Fluff
Top-tier writers use word counts to perform **"Fat-Cutting"**. 
1. Write your initial draft without limits.
2. Note the word count (e.g., 1,200 words).
3. Set a goal to reduce it by 20% (to 960 words) without losing information.
This process forces you to use stronger verbs and remove redundant adjectives, resulting in a "high-density" reading experience that keeps your audience engaged.
