Your One-Rep Max (1RM) is the maximum amount of weight you can lift for a single repetition of a given exercise with proper form. It is the gold standard for measuring absolute strength in movements like the squat, bench press, and deadlift.

## The Science of Estimation

Testing a true 1RM is taxing on the Central Nervous System (CNS) and requires significant recovery time. Because of this, most lifters use mathematical models to predict their 1RM based on "reps to failure" with lighter weights.

### The Epley Formula
Developed by Boyd Epley in 1985:  
**1RM = w * (1 + r / 30)**  
*(where w is weight and r is reps)*

### The Brzycki Formula
Developed by Matt Brzycki:  
**1RM = w / (1.0278 - (0.0278 * r))**

## Training with Percentages

Once you know your 1RM, you can structure your training sessions using percentages of that max to achieve specific goals:

- **Hypertrophy (Muscle Growth)**: 65% – 85% of 1RM
- **Absolute Strength**: 85% – 100% of 1RM
- **Power**: 50% – 60% of 1RM (moved with high velocity)
- **Endurance**: < 60% of 1RM

## Important Limitation

Strength estimation becomes significantly less accurate as repetitions increase. A 3-rep max estimation is very reliable, while a 15-rep max estimation has a high margin of error because cardiovascular endurance becomes a limiting factor before muscular strength.
