### How GPS Speed Calculation Works
Unlike a car's speedometer, which measures the rotation of the tires, a GPS speedometer uses **Satellite Triangulation**. 
1. **Distance/Time Method**: The receiver measures the time it takes to move between two sets of coordinates.
2. **Doppler Shift**: For higher precision, modern chips measure the change in frequency of the satellite signal caused by your movement (similar to how a siren changes pitch as it passes you).
Our tool uses the most advanced browser APIs to access this raw telemetry, providing a "Speed Over Ground" (SOG) measurement that is unaffected by tire wear or mechanical calibration errors.

### Factors Affecting Accuracy
While GPS is incredibly accurate, certain environmental factors can interfere with the signal:
*   **Urban Canyons**: Tall buildings can reflect signals, leading to "GPS Drift."
*   **Tunnels/Overpasses**: Direct line-of-sight to at least 4 satellites is required for a 3D lock.
*   **Weather**: While clouds don't stop GPS, extreme solar flares or heavy electrical storms can occasionally introduce minor jitter.
