## Human Velocity: Quantifying Your Running Performance
Running is the purest form of human movement. Whether you are training for a 5K, perfecting your marathon pace, or testing your top-end sprint speed, the Calcuva **Running GPS Speedometer** provides the live data you need to optimize your stride.

### The Mechanics of Speed: Cadence and Stride Length
Your running speed is a simple equation: **Stride Length × Stride Frequency**. 
*   **The Sprint**: Use our tool to measure your "Peak Velocity" during interval training.
*   **The Jog**: Monitor your average speed to ensure you are staying in the correct "Heart Rate Zone" for recovery or endurance.
*   **Trip Distance**: Track your total mileage with sub-meter precision.

### Expert Strategy: Mastering the "Negative Split"
The most effective way to run a race is the **Negative Split**—running the second half faster than the first. 
1.  **Monitor Your Average**: Use the first half of your run to establish a comfortable baseline.
2.  **The Surge**: Use the live speedometer to gradually increase your velocity by 0.5 - 1.0 km/h for the final miles.
3.  **Data Feedback**: Our high-contrast display makes it easy to check your stats mid-stride without breaking your form.

### Why GPS Beats the Pedometer
Old-school pedometers count steps and "estimate" distance based on a fixed stride length. 
*   **The Flaw**: Your stride length changes as you get tired or move uphill. 
*   **The GPS Solution**: Calcuva measures your actual movement over the ground. If you take shorter steps, the GPS doesn't care—it still knows exactly how fast you are moving and how far you've gone.

### Case Study: Sprint Performance and "Top End" Speed
Did you know that Usain Bolt reached a top speed of 27.78 mph? While most humans cruise at 6-8 mph, testing your "Top End" speed is a great way to build explosive power. 
*   **The Test**: Find a flat 100-meter stretch of pavement. 
*   **The Run**: Hit "Start" and accelerate to your maximum velocity. 
*   **The Result**: Check your **Max Speed** stat. Tracking this over months is the best way to see the results of your plyometric and strength training.
