### The Mechanics of Vertical Velocity
Elevator speed is measured differently than ground transport. 
*   **FPM (Feet Per Minute)**: While cars use mph, lifts use fpm. A "Fast" residential lift might do 500 fpm, while the world's fastest elevators can exceed 3,000 fpm (over 35 mph!).
*   **Acceleration and Jerk**: The "feeling" of the elevator isn't just the speed; it's how quickly it reaches that speed. Our **G-Force Tracker** monitors the transition from standstill to full velocity.

### Understanding G-Force in High-Rise Buildings
Why do some elevators make your stomach drop while others feel like you aren't moving at all? 
*   **Acceleration Phase**: As the lift pulls away, the force increases. 1.1G to 1.2G is common for high-performance lifts.
*   **Steady State**: Once the lift is at its "Terminal Velocity," you should feel exactly 1.0G (normal Earth gravity).
*   **Deceleration**: As the lift approaches its floor, the G-force drops, often to 0.8G or 0.9G, giving you that brief feeling of weightlessness.
