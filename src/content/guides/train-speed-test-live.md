### The Physics of Rail Speed
Trains move differently than cars. They maintain high "Steady State" speeds for long durations. 
*   **Constant Velocity**: Our tool tracks average speed over long intervals, allowing you to see how efficiently the train maintains its cruise.
*   **Grade and Curve Impact**: Watch how the speed dips as the train navigates mountain passes or tight curves, then surges as it hits a straightaway.
*   **Distance Logging**: Track exactly how many kilometers you've covered, a perfect metric for travel bloggers and rail enthusiasts.

### Global Benchmarks: What Speed to Expect?
How does your current ride compare to the world's elite rail systems?
*   **320 km/h (200 mph)**: The standard "Cruise Speed" for the Japanese Shinkansen and French TGV.
*   **250 km/h (155 mph)**: The threshold for most European "Intercity" high-speed lines.
*   **160 km/h (100 mph)**: Fast "Commuter Rail" or regional express services.

### Why Train Speedometers Vary
You may notice that the official display at the end of the train car shows a different speed than your phone. 
*   **Update Frequency**: Train displays often show "Buffered" data to avoid rapid flickering. 
*   **Wheel Diameter**: Like cars, train sensors can be off if the wheels are worn. 
*   **GPS Precision**: Calcuva uses **Ground Speed**, which is the most accurate measurement of your actual displacement over the Earth.
