Unlike basic apps, our tool focuses on high-frequency velocity tracking. 
*   **Live Velocity**: Real-time tracking in mph or km/h.
*   **Trip Distance**: Accurate to within a few meters, allowing you to track your endurance progress.
*   **Average Pace**: Monitor your efficiency over long flats and challenging climbs.

### Expert Strategy: Optimizing Your Cycling Speed
To increase your average speed, you need to monitor your "Steady State" velocity. 
1.  **Drafting**: Use the live speedometer to see the immediate 2-3 mph gain when tucking behind another rider.
2.  **Cadence vs. Speed**: Track how different gear ratios affect your top speed on the same stretch of road.
3.  **Climb Management**: Use the distance tracker to pace yourself on long ascents, ensuring you don't "blow up" before the summit.

### GPS vs. Traditional Magnet Sensors
Old-school bike computers use a magnet on the spoke and a sensor on the fork. 
*   **The Problem**: If your tire pressure drops, the wheel's circumference changes, making the computer read high.
*   **The GPS Solution**: Calcuva uses satellite displacement. It doesn't care about your tires; it only cares about your movement across the earth. This makes it the perfect tool for riders who swap between different wheelsets or bikes.
