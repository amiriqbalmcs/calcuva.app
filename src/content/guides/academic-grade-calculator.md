#### Understanding the Weighted Average System
Most modern courses do not use a simple average. Instead, they use a **Weighted System** where different assignments have different levels of importance.
*   **Homework/Quizzes**: Often 20-30% of your grade.
*   **Midterm Exams**: Typically 20-30%.
*   **Final Exam**: Usually the most significant, ranging from 30% to 50%.
The weight tells you how much "power" an assignment has over your final result. A high score on a low-weight quiz is good, but a high score on a high-weight exam is game-changing.

#### The Final Exam Forecaster: Reverse-Engineering Success
The most stressful part of the semester is "Finals Week." Most students ask: *"What do I need to get on the final to keep my A?"* 
Our calculator solves this using the **Final Grade Formula**:
`Required Final Score = [Target Grade - (Current Grade * Current Weight)] / Final Weight`
By knowing your required score in advance, you can prioritize your study time. If you only need a 65% to keep your B, you can spend more time studying for the difficult class where you need a 92%.

#### Expert Strategy: The "Buffer" Method
Don't just aim for your target grade; aim for a **2% buffer**. 
1. If your target is 90% (A), set our calculator to **92%**.
2. This accounts for small errors, missed points, or a particularly difficult exam paper.
3. If you hit your "Buffered" goal, you've secured your A. If you fall slightly short, you still land exactly where you needed to be.

#### Technical Insight: Managing Totals
A common mistake in grade tracking is ignoring the **Remaining Weight**. If you have completed 60% of the course work and have a 90% average, you have "earned" 54 points toward your final grade (90 x 0.60). 
The remaining 46 points (from the 100% total) are still "up for grabs." Our tool tracks this "Point Pool" automatically, showing you exactly how much of your final grade is already secured.

#### Why "Points" can be Deceiving
In a point-based system (e.g., 1000 total points in a class), every point feels equal. However, teachers often group these into categories. 50 points in the "Participation" category might be worth more than 100 points in the "Extra Credit" category if the weights are different. **Always prioritize the category with the highest percentage weight.**
