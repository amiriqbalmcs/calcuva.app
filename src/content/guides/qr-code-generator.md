QR codes have become the bridge between the physical and digital worlds. From restaurant menus to payment systems, these two-dimensional barcodes allow for instant data transfer without the friction of typing.

## How They Work: Error Correction

One of the most impressive features of a QR code is **Error Correction**. Because of the Reed-Solomon mathematical algorithm, a QR code can be scanned even if it is partially damaged or obscured (up to 30% damage in some cases). This is why you can sometimes see logos in the center of QR codes without breaking them.

## Static vs. Dynamic QR Codes

- **Static QR Codes**: The data is hardcoded into the pattern. They are free, permanent, and don't require a middle-man. (This is what Calcuva generates).
- **Dynamic QR Codes**: The code links to a redirect server that then sends the user to the final destination. This allows you to track scans and change the URL later, but usually requires a paid subscription.

## Best Practices for Printing

1.  **Contrast is Key**: Always use a dark foreground on a light background. Scanning a "negative" QR code (light on dark) is difficult for many apps.
2.  **Size Matters**: Ensure the code is at least 2cm x 2cm for printed materials. The more data you encode (like a very long URL), the denser the pattern becomes, requiring a larger print size to be readable.
3.  **Test Before You Print**: Always scan your generated code with multiple different devices and apps before committing it to a large print run.
