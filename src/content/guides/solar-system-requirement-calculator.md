Choosing the right solar system size is the most critical step in your transition to renewable energy. A system that is too small won't cover your bills, while one that is too large may result in a longer ROI period due to lower export rates.

## Step 1: Inventory Your Load
The first step is to list all the appliances you intend to run on solar. For each appliance, you need to know:
- **Wattage**: The power it consumes (found on the sticker behind the appliance).
- **Quantity**: How many of these you have.
- **Usage Hours**: How many hours per day you run them.

## Step 2: Calculate Daily Energy (kWh)
Multiply the wattage by quantity and hours for each appliance, then sum them up. Divide by 1000 to get the daily units (kWh) required.

## Step 3: Account for Losses
Solar systems aren't 100% efficient. Inverters, wiring, and dust on panels can reduce efficiency by 15-25%. Our calculator uses an 80% efficiency factor by default.

## Step 4: The Peak Sun Hours Rule
The energy produced depends on the intensity and duration of sunlight. In Pakistan, we are blessed with high irradiance, averaging 5.5 peak sun hours. 

**Formula**: 
`Required kW = Daily Units / (Peak Sun Hours × Efficiency)`

## Pro Tip for 2026
With the new fixed charges on net-metering in Pakistan, it is often better to size your system for **70-80% of your total load** rather than 100%, especially if you don't have batteries. This maximizes self-consumption and avoids paying high fixed charges for a large connection.
