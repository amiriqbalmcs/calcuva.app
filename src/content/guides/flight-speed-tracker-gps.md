### Ground Speed vs. Air Speed: The Pilot's Secret
One of the most common questions in aviation is: *"Why are we arriving early?"*
*   **Air Speed**: How fast the plane is moving through the air around it.
*   **Ground Speed (GS)**: How fast the plane is moving relative to the earth's surface. 
*   **The Difference**: If a plane has an airspeed of 850 km/h but is flying into a 100 km/h "Jet Stream" headwind, its ground speed is only 750 km/h. Conversely, a tailwind can push you to supersonic ground speeds. Our tool measures **Ground Speed**, the only metric that determines your actual arrival time.

Our Flight Tracker doesn't just measure velocity; it monitors your **Altitude**. 
*   **The Ascent**: Watch the altitude climb from 0 to 35,000 feet in real-time.
*   **The Cruise**: Monitor how the pilot adjusts altitude to find smoother air or better winds.
*   **Vertical Speed**: Our engine tracks the rate of descent as you approach your destination, giving you a heads-up before the "descending" announcement is even made.

### Expert Strategy: Getting a GPS Lock at High Altitude
Airplane cabins are essentially pressurized metal tubes, which can make it hard for GPS signals to penetrate. 
1.  **The Window Rule**: You **must** be in a window seat.
2.  **Glass Proximity**: Hold your phone directly against the window for 30-60 seconds to "capture" the initial satellite constellation.
3.  **Stability**: Once locked, the phone can usually maintain the signal even if moved slightly away from the glass.

At cruising altitude, the air is thin, and the engines are at their most efficient.
