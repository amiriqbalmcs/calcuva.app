## Solar ROI in 2026: Navigating the New Policy Landscape

Solar energy remains the most effective way for Pakistani households to hedge against rising electricity costs. However, with the 2025-26 policy changes, calculating your **Return on Investment (ROI)** has become more complex.

### Factors Affecting Your Solar Savings

1. **Rising Tariffs**: With base unit prices exceeding Rs. 55, every unit you generate and consume at home saves you the full retail price.
2. **Net-Metering Slabs**: NEPRA's new buyback rates mean you earn less for what you "Export" than what you "Save" through direct use.
3. **Fixed Charges**: The introduction of fixed monthly charges per kW of solar capacity has slightly extended the payback period, but the overall ROI remains high.

### Understanding the Payback Period

The "Payback Period" is the time it takes for your electricity savings to equal the initial cost of your solar system. 
- **Higher Consumption = Faster Payback**: The more you use solar during the day (for ACs, water pumps), the faster your ROI.
- **System Efficiency**: Our tool assumes a 20% system loss (dust, inverter efficiency, wiring), which provides a realistic 2026 estimation.

### Pro Tip for 2026
Focus on **Load Shifting**. By moving your heavy appliance usage (Washing machines, ACs) to peak sunlight hours (11 AM - 3 PM), you maximize your direct savings and avoid the lower export credit rates. Use our **Self-Consumption Slider** to see how this affects your monthly bill.
