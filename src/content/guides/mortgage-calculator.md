Buying a home is often the largest financial transaction of a person's life. Understanding the mathematics behind your mortgage loan is crucial to ensuring you are getting a fair deal and making strategic payoff decisions.

## What is Amortization?

Amortization is the process of spreading out a loan into a series of fixed payments over time. You are paying off both the principal and the interest simultaneously. 

Although your monthly payment (often called EMI - Equated Monthly Installment) remains exactly the same every month for the life of a fixed-rate loan, the *ratio* of Principal to Interest within that payment changes constantly.

### The Amortization Schedule
1. **Early Years:** Mostly Interest. For a typical 30-year mortgage, the first 5-10 years consist mostly of paying interest to the bank. 
2. **The Tipping Point:** Usually around halfway through the loan term, the lines cross, and you begin paying more toward your principal than interest each month.
3. **Late Years:** Mostly Principal. The balance is small, so the interest charge is small, and nearly the entirety of your payment goes to wiping out the remaining debt.

## Strategies to Save on Interest

- **15-Year vs 30-Year:** A 15-year mortgage will have significantly higher monthly payments, but you will pay drastically less in total lifetime interest, often a difference of hundreds of thousands of dollars.
- **Bi-Weekly Payments:** By paying half your mortgage every two weeks instead of once a month, you end up making 26 half-payments in a year, which equals 13 full payments. That one extra payment per year directly hits the principal and shaves years off your loan.
- **Lump Sum Injections:** Applying an annual bonus or tax refund directly to the principal balance yields massive lifetime interest savings.
