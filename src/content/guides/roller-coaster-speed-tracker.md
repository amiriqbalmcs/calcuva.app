### The Two Pillars of Intensity: Speed and G-Force
To truly understand a roller coaster's performance, you need to track two distinct datasets:
1.  **Velocity (Speed)**: Measured via GPS, this tells you exactly how fast you hit the bottom of the first drop.
2.  **Intensity (G-Force)**: Measured via the accelerometer, this tells you how much 'Weight' you felt during the turns. 

### Understanding G-Force: Positive vs. Negative
When you ride a coaster, you experience different types of force:
*   **Positive Gs (The Crush)**: Felt at the bottom of a drop or in a tight loop. It makes you feel several times heavier than normal as you are pushed into your seat.
*   **Negative Gs (Airtime)**: The "stomach-drop" feeling at the crest of a hill. This is when the G-force drops below 1.0, making you feel weightless.
*   **Lateral Gs**: The side-to-side force felt in unbanked turns. 
Our tool tracks **Peak G-Force**, recording the single most intense moment of your ride for you to analyze later.

### Why GPS Speed is Critical for Coaster Buffs
Did you know that coasters actually run at different speeds depending on the weather and the "train weight"? 
*   **Morning vs. Afternoon**: Coasters often run "faster" in the afternoon as the grease in the wheel bearings heats up and becomes less viscous.
*   **Full Train vs. Empty Train**: A fully loaded train has more momentum and will often hit higher top speeds than a test run.
