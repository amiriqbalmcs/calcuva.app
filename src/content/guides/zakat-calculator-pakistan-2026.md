## Understanding Zakat in 2026

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam, a mandatory charitable contribution of 2.5% of one's accumulated wealth. In modern financial systems, calculating Zakat requires assessing various asset classes from crypto to real estate.

### What Assets are Zakatable?

1. **Precious Metals:** Gold and Silver in any form (jewelry, coins, or bars).
2. **Cash:** Money in hand, in bank accounts, or digital wallets (EasyPaisa/JazzCash).
3. **Investments:** Stocks, Mutual Funds, Sukuks, and Cryptocurrency.
4. **Business Assets:** Trading inventory and raw materials.
5. **Money Lent Out:** Loans you have given to others that you expect to be repaid.

### The Nisab Threshold

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a person must possess before they are obligated to pay Zakat. 
- **Gold Nisab:** 87.48 grams (7.5 Tolas)
- **Silver Nisab:** 612.36 grams (52.5 Tolas)

In 2026, the Silver Nisab is the standard used by most welfare organizations in Pakistan (like Edhi and Saylani) as it allows more people to contribute to the social safety net.

### How to Calculate Your Zakat

The formula is simple:
**[Total Zakatable Assets] - [Eligible Debts] = Net Wealth**
If **Net Wealth > Nisab**, then:
**Zakat = Net Wealth × 0.025**

### Deduction of Zakat at Source (Banks)

In Pakistan, banks often deduct Zakat (2.5%) automatically from Saving Accounts on the 1st of Ramadan. If you have already paid this or are exempt (e.g., following a specific school of thought), ensure you have submitted a **CZ-50 affidavit** to your bank before Ramadan starts.
